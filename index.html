About Me

I am a Ph. D. student in the school of civil and environmental engineering at the University of New South Wales and investigates climate modeling experiments to evaluate the extent of the change resulting in extreme precipitation. I have interested in hydrology, hydrological model, atmospheric science, bias correction, climate model, and climate change.

Current project: The rise in global average temperature not only has a direct impact on the environment, such as changes in sea level rise and precipitation extremes but also has a variety of impacts on human life, including water, energy, agriculture, food. Therefore, relevant research is necessary to cope with such changes, and appropriate measures for adaptation to water resources should be established. My research is to analyse the intensification of the storm caused by the increase in temperature due to climate change. These results can help establish a framework to design civil engineering infrastructures in warmer climates.

I have been actively involved in governmental projects and programs. I completed to develop an integrated water resource model that was funded by the Korean Ministry of Environment. This task was not merely to conduct research on water resources but to carry out multidisciplinary studies on aspects such as health, water management, environment, ecology, forestry and climate, and continuously discuss with professional researchers in order to integrate all fields in the outcomes that were formulated.

I have coding experience with Python, Fotran, R, and MATLAB.
